# Department Head User Manual
## ALPAS Time & Attendance System

As a Department Head, you have all Employee features **plus** the ability to approve or reject your team's requests. This guide covers your additional responsibilities.

> **Also see:** The Employee Manual covers Login, Clock In/Out, Leave Filing, Overtime, Profile, and Notifications — all of which apply to you as well.

## 1. Your Role Overview

You are the **first approver** for your team's requests. HR may perform a final review after you for certain request types.

**What you can approve:**
- Leave requests from your team
- Overtime filing requests from your team
- Attendance correction requests from your team
- Overtime conversion requests (CTO/CDO) from your team

**What you cannot approve:**
- Your own requests (these go directly to HR)
- Requests from employees in other departments

**Navigation:** Sidebar → **Approvals**

The Approvals page has four tabs at the top. Click each tab to switch between request types:

---

### Leaves Tab

**What you will see:** Pending leave requests from your team, showing employee name, leave type, dates, number of days, and reason.

**Steps:**
1. Go to **Approvals** and click the **Leaves** tab.
2. Review the request details.
3. Click **Approve** or **Reject**.
4. A small window appears. Enter **notes** (required when rejecting; optional when approving).
5. Click **Submit**.
6. The employee is notified automatically.

> **Note:** Your own leave requests will not appear here — they go to HR directly.

[Insert Screenshot: Leave Approvals Tab]

---

### Overtime Tab

**What you will see:** Filed overtime requests from your team, showing employee name, date, hours, and reason.

**Steps:**
1. Click the **Overtime** tab.
2. Review the hours and reason.
3. Click **Approve** or **Reject**, add notes if needed, and click **Submit**.

> **Note:** Approving an overtime request credits the employee with those hours. Credits are valid for 6 months.

[Insert Screenshot: Overtime Approvals Tab]

---

### Corrections Tab

**What you will see:** Attendance correction requests from your team, showing the date, requested clock-in time, requested clock-out time, and reason.

**Steps:**
1. Click the **Corrections** tab.
2. Review the requested times and the reason carefully.
3. If you approve, the attendance record is updated automatically to the corrected times.
4. Click **Approve** or **Reject**, add notes if rejecting, and click **Submit**.

> **Tip:** If a correction shows an overnight shift (clock-out time later than midnight), verify the dates make sense before approving.

[Insert Screenshot: Corrections Approvals Tab]

---

### OT Conversions Tab

**What you will see:** Requests from your team to convert overtime credits to CTO (Compensatory Time Off) or CDO (Credit Day Off).

**Steps:**
1. Click the **OT Conversions** tab.
2. Review the conversion type, scheduled date, and number of hours.
3. Click **Approve** or **Reject** with notes.

**Minimum hours required:**
- CTO: at least **4 hours**
- CDO: at least **8 hours**

Requests below the minimum should be rejected.

[Insert Screenshot: OT Conversions Approvals Tab]

---

### Viewing Review History

On each tab, toggle **"Show Review History"** to see requests you have already approved or rejected.

## 4. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: A request from my team is not appearing in my queue.**
A: Your own requests are routed to HR and will not appear in your queue. Also check that the employee's department is correctly set to yours.

**Q: Can I reverse an approval I already made?**
A: No. Once approved or rejected, the decision is final in the system. Contact HR for manual corrections.

**Q: An employee's hours are showing as negative on the dashboard.**
A: Ask the employee to file an attendance correction and use the "Clock-out is on the next day (overnight)" checkbox. When you receive the correction request, verify the times and approve if correct.

**Q: A team member says they cannot see a request they filed.**
A: Drafts (unfiled overtime records) are private to the employee until they click "File OT". If the record is older than 15 days, it may have already expired.
